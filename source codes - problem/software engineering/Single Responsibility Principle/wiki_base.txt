
== Intro ==

Guild Holdings Company (GHLD) is a mortgage company that focuses on providing a personalized mortgage-borrowing experience to its clients. They employ a relationship-based loan sourcing strategy and aim to deliver the promise of homeownership in neighborhoods and communities across the United States. GHLD generates revenue primarily through its origination segment, with the retail channel accounting for approximately 95% of the origination segment in 2023. They earn revenue through gain on sale and fees associated with originating and selling mortgage loans to the secondary market. Furthermore, GHLD benefits from a balanced business model that includes both origination and servicing segments, allowing them to navigate through interest rate and market cycles effectively[0].

GHLD’s main expertise lies in its origination strategy, which focuses on increasing its purchase-mortgage business. This strategy is executed through a combination of experienced loan officers, a technology platform, and diverse product offerings. They have developed an in-house servicing platform that creates opportunities to extend relationships with clients and generate refinance and purchase volume. Additionally, GHLD has a proprietary technology platform that underpins loan officer productivity, fosters repeat business, and enables them to control the lending process from start to finish. Their technology platform has been developed over the course of their long operating history and is primarily proprietary, developed internally by their own employees[1][2].

In summary, GHLD is a mortgage company that specializes in providing a personalized mortgage-borrowing experience, generating revenue through mortgage loan origination, and leveraging technology and expertise to enhance client relationships and drive business growth. Their core strengths include a focus on purchase-mortgage business, an in-house servicing platform, and a proprietary technology platform that supports their loan officers and client interactions.


=== Cost ===

Guild Holdings Company (GHLD) incurs various costs across different segments of its operations. Some key cost segments include:

# Salaries, incentive compensation, and benefits: This cost segment covers salaries, incentives, and benefits for employees. In the year ended December 31, 2023, this expense accounted for a significant portion of the total expenses in both the origination and servicing segments of GHLD.
# General and administrative expenses: This segment includes general overhead costs related to the administration of the company. These expenses increased in the year ended December 31, 2023, compared to the previous year, due to specific factors like valuation adjustments and other administrative costs.
# Occupancy, equipment, and communication: Costs related to occupancy, equipment, and communication are part of GHLD’s expense structure. These costs include office rental expenses, equipment purchases, and communication services. The expenses in this segment remained relatively stable during the year ended December 31, 2023, reflecting expected changes associated with acquisitions and operational adjustments.
# Depreciation and amortization: This cost segment represents the expenses related to the depreciation of assets and the amortization of intangible assets. The depreciation and amortization expenses remained relatively consistent from year to year, reflecting the nature of the assets being depreciated and amortized.
# Provision for foreclosure losses: GHLD sets aside reserves for potential losses related to foreclosures. The company monitors foreclosure reserves regularly and assesses the need for adjustments based on risk factors and market conditions.

By understanding and managing these cost segments effectively, GHLD can optimize its operational efficiency and financial performance in its origination and servicing segments[0][1].
